## 1. The First-Time International Master’s Student – Aarya Sharma

**Objective:**
Aarya wants to find affordable public universities in Germany that offer English-taught Cybersecurity programs.

**Actors:**
Aarya Sharma: A 22-year-old international student from India who is planning to pursue a master’s degree in Germany. She has limited knowledge about German universities and relies on online resources for information.

**Activities:**
Aarya opens the International Student Assistant web application and navigates to the University Finder section. She applies filters based on his preferences:

* Course: Cybersecurity
* Language: English
* Tuition: Public universities

The system processes the selected filters and displays a list of universities along with details such as location, program information, and estimated living costs. She compares the available options and creates a shortlist of universities that match his academic goals and budget.

**Problem/Gap:**

Currently, shee is facing the problem of finding the information about German universities as it is spread across multiple websites, making it difficult and time-consuming for international students to compare programs, tuition types, and language options efficiently.

**Resolution:**

The International Student Assistant simplifies the process by centralizing university information into a single platform with filtering functionality. This allows Aarya to quickly find suitable universities based on her preferences, saving time and reducing confusion during the application process.

## 2. The Newly Arrived Student – Sita Gurung

**Objective:**
Sita wants to successfully complete her city registration (Anmeldung) after arriving in Germany.

**Actors:**
Sita Gurung: A 24-year-old international student from Nepal who has recently arrived in Germany for her master’s studies. She is unfamiliar with German administrative procedures and has limited understanding of the German language.

**Activities:**
After arriving in Germany, Sita needs to complete her Anmeldung to legally register her residence. Unsure about the process, she opens the International Student Assistant web application and navigates to the Germany Setup Guide section.

She selects the “Anmeldung” option, where the system provides a structured step-by-step guide containing:

* Required documents
* Office location and process information
* Expectations during the appointment

Sita reviews the guide carefully, gathers all required documents, and prepares herself before visiting the registration office.

**Problem/Gap:**

International students often struggle with German bureaucratic procedures because information is scattered across different websites and official instructions are difficult to understand, especially for non-German speakers. This can lead to confusion, missed documents, and delayed registration.

**Resolution:**

The International Student Assistant centralizes and simplifies the Anmeldung process by providing clear, easy-to-follow guidance in one place. This helps Sita understand the procedure, prepare the correct documents, and complete her registration successfully without unnecessary stress or mistakes.

**Problem/Gap:**

Accommodation information for international students is often scattered across different platforms, making it difficult and time-consuming to compare housing types and living costs efficiently. Students with busy schedules may struggle to make informed decisions quickly.

**Resolution:**

The International Student Assistant simplifies the accommodation research process by presenting essential housing information in a centralized and easy-to-understand format. This allows Lucas to quickly evaluate his options, save time, and make a more informed housing decision based on his financial situation.
